Panic Disorder

Panic disorder is a type of anxiety disorder characterized by recurring unexpected panic attacks. These attacks are sudden periods of intense fear or discomfort that reach a peak within minutes and may include symptoms such as a racing heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, and feelings of impending doom.

Symptoms

  • Rapid heartbeat or palpitations
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Feeling of choking
  • Fear of losing control or "going crazy"
  • Fear of dying

Causes

The exact cause of panic disorder is not known, but several factors may contribute, including genetics, brain chemistry, and stressful life events. Individuals with a family history of anxiety disorders may also be at a higher risk.

Treatment

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • Medications such as antidepressants or benzodiazepines
  • Support groups and lifestyle changes, such as regular exercise and relaxation techniques
